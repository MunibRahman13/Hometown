About Patna

Patna is the capital and largest city of the Indian state of Bihar. It is one of the oldest continuously inhabited places in the world. The city has a rich history and is known for its cultural heritage, historical monuments, and vibrant lifestyle.

History

Patna has a long and illustrious history dating back thousands of years. It was originally known as Pataliputra and served as the capital of several ancient Indian kingdoms, including the Magadha Empire and the Maurya Empire.

During the Maurya period, under the rule of Emperor Ashoka, Pataliputra became a major center of trade, politics, and culture in ancient India. It was also an important center for Buddhism and Jainism. The famous Mauryan Pillars, including the iconic Ashoka Pillar, were erected in Pataliputra during this period.

Over the centuries, Patna continued to thrive as a center of power and influence in the region. It was ruled by various dynasties, including the Guptas, the Palas, and the Mughals, each leaving their mark on the city's architecture and culture.

In the 16th century, Patna became an important center of trade and commerce during the Mughal period. The city flourished as a hub for the silk and spice trade, attracting merchants from across Asia and Europe.

In the colonial era, Patna became an important center of British administration in India. It played a significant role in the Indian independence movement and was witness to many important events during that period. The iconic Gandhi Maidan in Patna was the site of several key events during India's struggle for independence.

After independence, Patna continued to grow and develop as an important urban center in post-colonial India. It emerged as a major educational, cultural, and commercial hub in the region, attracting people from all walks of life.

Today, Patna is a vibrant and dynamic city that blends its rich historical legacy with modernity. It is home to numerous historical monuments, cultural institutions, and educational centers, making it a fascinating destination for visitors and a proud home for its residents.

Did You Know?

Ancient City: Patna, formerly known as Pataliputra, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Its history dates back to ancient times, and it was the capital of several major Indian kingdoms, including the Magadha Empire and the Maurya Empire.

Educational Hub: Patna is a major center for education in Bihar and Eastern India. It is home to several prestigious educational institutions, including Patna University, one of the oldest universities in the country, established in 1917.

Political Importance: Being the capital city of Bihar, Patna holds significant political importance. It serves as the administrative, legislative, and judicial capital of the state.

Religious Diversity: Patna is known for its religious diversity. It is home to various religious sites, including the renowned Takht Sri Patna Sahib, one of the holiest sites in Sikhism, where Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth Sikh Guru, was born.

Cultural Heritage: The city boasts a rich cultural heritage, with influences from various historical periods. Its cultural diversity is reflected in its festivals, cuisine, music, and arts.

Historical Monuments: Patna is home to several historical monuments and landmarks, including Golghar, Patna Museum, Agam Kuan, and the ruins of Pataliputra, which attract tourists and history enthusiasts from around the world.

Attractions

Some popular attractions in Patna include:

  • Mahatma Gandhi Setu: This bridge over the River Ganges is one of the longest river bridges in India.
  • Patna Museum (Jadu Ghar): Established in 1917, this museum houses a vast collection of artifacts.
  • Takht Sri Patna Sahib: Also known as Harmandir Sahib, this is one of the holiest Sikh shrines.
  • Kumhrar: This archaeological site is believed to be the location of Pataliputra, the ancient capital.
  • Agam Kuan: Translated as "Unfathomable Well," Agam Kuan is an ancient well dating back to the Maurya dynasty.
  • Golghar: Built by the British in the 18th century, Golghar is a massive granary that offers a panoramic view of the city.
  • Patna Zoo (Sanjay Gandhi Jaivik Udyan): Spread over a large area, this zoo is home to various species of animals, birds, and reptiles.
  • Bihar Museum: This modern museum showcases the history, art, and culture of Bihar.
  • Padri Ki Haveli: This historic church is one of the oldest churches in Bihar, dating back to the early 18th century.
  • Eco Park: Located near Gandhi Maidan, this park is a popular recreational spot for locals and tourists alike.
